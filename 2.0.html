Strategy overview

Research question

When the order book shows extreme negative imbalance and the bid-ask spread widens, does price reverse or continue?

Why this is microstructure-based

Order book imbalance reflects visible liquidity pressure. Spread widening reflects worse immediacy and higher trading friction.

OBI ↓
+
Spread ↑
→
Liquidity stress

Two competing interpretations

Reversal?

Initial idea: liquidity shock may cause temporary overreaction, followed by a short-term rebound.

Continuation.

Empirical result: OOS execution-aware returns are significantly negative, so pressure continues after the signal.

Market microstructure variables
Key variables

      

        
VariableDefinitionInterpretation

          
Mid-price(best ask + best bid) / 2Reference quote price

          
Relative spread(ask − bid) / mid-priceLiquidity cost and immediacy friction

          
OBI(bid depth − ask depth) / total depthVisible pressure across five order-book levels

          
OFI proxyChange in best bid queue − change in best ask queueSimplified short-term queue-pressure proxy

          
Micro-priceDepth-weighted quote priceImbalance-adjusted reference price

        

      

      

        
Core signal inputs
OBI captures order-book pressure. Relative spread captures liquidity deterioration. Their joint occurrence is interpreted as liquidity stress.

        
Important wording
We use a simplified OFI proxy, not the full classical price-change-adjusted OFI.

      

    


    

      

    


    

      
Methodology
Signal construction and OOS design

      

      

        

          
Main signal
Signal = 1 if
OBI_5 < P10(OBI_5, in-sample)
and
Relative Spread > P90(Spread, in-sample)
Negative OBI plus wide spread is interpreted as a liquidity-stress event.

          
70% / 30% split
In-sample: estimate thresholds
Out-of-sample: generate signals and evaluate returns
This improves statistical credibility and reduces overfitting.

          
No future leakage
Decision at time t uses only information visible at time t.
Future quotes are used only for return evaluation.
This is critical for high-frequency backtesting.

        

        

          
What the technical speaker should emphasize

          

            
Thresholds are in-sample onlyNo future OOS data is used to calibrate the signal.

            
Signals are OOS onlyThe empirical results are not fitted on the same data.

            
Multiple horizons5s, 10s, 30s, and 60s are all tested.

          

        

      

    


    

      

    


    

      

    


    

      
Interpretation
What did the data actually reject?

      

        
Original reversal interpretation
Negative OBI + wide spread may represent temporary overreaction. If true, execution-aware returns after the signal should be positive.

        
Empirical continuation interpretation
Execution-aware returns are negative and statistically significant. The shock is not finished; price pressure continues.

      

      

      

        
OOS execution returns
Average execution-aware returns are mostly negative across stocks and horizons. This contradicts immediate mean reversion.

        
10-second t-test
The 10-second execution returns are significantly negative. The opposite direction is not just random noise.

        
Core finding
The signal is informative, but the information is in the continuation direction rather than the reversal direction.

      

    


    

      
Robustness, limitations, future work
Critical evaluation

      

        
📌
Limited stock universe
Only three A-share stocks. Future work should test a broader universe and market-cap groups.

        
🧪
Simplified OFI proxy
We use queue-volume changes, not full price-change-adjusted OFI.

        
💸
Execution model
Entry at ask and exit at bid is conservative, but market impact and queue priority are still simplified.

        
🔁
Natural extension
Test a short-side continuation strategy on a new OOS sample to avoid hindsight fitting.

      

      
Why this still works as a project
The project demonstrates hypothesis testing, OOS design, transaction-cost-aware thinking, and critical interpretation of microstructure evidence.

    


    

      
Final conclusion
Conclusion: continuation rather than reversal

      
Reversal hypothesis rejected.
Continuation supported.
OBI + spread widening captures liquidity stress, but the stress continues instead of immediately reversing in our A-share sample.

      

        
Why not short it?
Natural future work; must test on new OOS data to avoid data mining.

        
Why no annualized return?
Second-level event strategy; per-trade execution return and t-test are more appropriate.

        
Is OFI standard?
No. It is a simplified queue-pressure proxy, acknowledged as a limitation.
